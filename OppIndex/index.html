The Geography of Educational Equity in King County, WA.

Are we living in a world where all people have the same access to resources and opportunities? In the contemporary world, not all people live in the same condition. Some people have more and easier access to the resources and opportunities while some others barely have access. Therefore, it is important to understand the true meaning of social equity.

According to the report from the Puget Sound Regional Council (2012), “social equity means that all people, regardless of where they live, have access to the resources and opportunities that improve their quality of life…” (p. 3). Without having social equity for all people, the culture of rich get richer and the poor get poorer will continuously reside within our world..

To break this culture, the first step should be helping children to get the same educational opportunity. With the educational opportunity maps, it will help determining areas with the higher and lower educational opportunity in King County.

Five Variables for the Opportunity Index

  • These maps depicts five variables that indicate the quality of local schools and educational resources. The variables are following: Washington Assessment of Student Learning (WASL) reading test scores, math test scores, student poverty rates, teacher qualifications, and graduation rates.
  • By using the Z-score method, the data were prepared to be calculated for the opportunity index.
  • However, many scholarly articles have found that the poverty rate tends to affect more in the children’s educational success (see Ferguson et al.). Therefore, the povery rate was weighted more than other four variables when processed through the Z-score method.
  • It is found that except for teacher qualifications, the other four maps share a similar geography. According to the four maps, it is found that the areas from the south of Seattle to Federal Way have mostly low and the very low opportunity.
  • One of the interesting findings from these maps is that regardless of opportunities, they are clustered.
  • Additionally, as the maps show the cluster of communities, it can be interpreted as that people tend to live within a similar groups of people. Or at least, they end up living in a similar neighborhood.
  • Another interesting finding is that teacher qualifications do not really have a positive correlation with graduation rate.
  • However, it is found that the poverty rate has more impacts in graduation rate as they share the same geography of educational opportunity, where the poverty rate is low, graduation rate is high. It may indicate children from affluent families have better educational opportunity as they may receive extra education outside of school by having a tutor whereas children living in poverty barely make it to school.


    • The Final Opportunity Index


  • The educational opportunity index map is a composite map that includes all the five variables. As previously explained, the Z-score values from all variables were combined into one to show the educational equity in King County.
  • The educational opportunity map shows that areas from Federal Way, Burien, SeaTac, Tukwila, and South Seattle have the low to the very low educational opportunity.
  • On contrary, North Seattle, Bellevue, Issaquah, and Maple Valley have the high to the very high opportunity. Vashon Island and Maury Island also have the high opportunity as they were in all the other individual maps.
  • Similar to the five individual maps, the composite map shows that educational opportunity is much lower in cities on the left side of King County. The problem of having clustered areas is that it continuously generates a bad cycle. See this report for more detail.


    • Conclusion

  • The question that was asked in the beginning now may be answered more clearly. No, we do not live in the world where all people have equal access to the educational equity, at least, not in King County.
  • The results from all the maps generated from this project clearly show some areas struggle more in having educational opportunities than some other areas.
  • As explained, the problem of clustered areas with low opportunity is that it cycles over and over again.
  • This project, therefore, shall help more people to notice the problem, and policymakers should use these maps to inform more people and consider them to make policies that can truly help marginalized communities and provide better opportunities to break the bad cycle.
